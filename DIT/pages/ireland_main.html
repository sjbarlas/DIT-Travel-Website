v Ireland
- " Céad míle fáilte romhat! "

If you're looking for great travel opportunities but are on a tight budget, there are plenty of destinations available on Irish soil. Great food, excellent hospitality and a wide range of cultural events all await you! Dublin, Galway and Cork host many popular attractions for holiday goers, without the need to travel very far to get to them! So why not take it easy and travel at home, read on to learn more about what what Ireland has to offer!

Places to Go & Things to See

There are many beautiful attractions in Ireland for hikers and travellers alike. The West Coast, Galway and Clare especially, have hillwalks and hikes that guide you along some of the rural sites to see. Views from the coast of the sea and fields of green are only some of the sites you will see. The Cliffs of Moher, Co. Clare, are a definite must see.

On the East coast, Co. Wicklow is number one on the list. Hailed as the 'Garden of Ireland' Wicklow is a popular destination for any sight-seer. Here Glendalough can be found, a beautiful open park with picturesque sights and trails for all levels of walker. Mount Usher Gardens, Ashford is another recommendation in the locality. Aside from the exotic gardens there is also an excellent restaurant and cafe. If sightseeing is not your thing, there are several other attractions for all ages. Dublin Zoo, one of the largest in Europe, is located just outside the city centre in Phoenix Park. The park itself is also worth a visit, the largest of its kind in Ireland and a perfect escape from the bustle of the city.
Dublin city centre is a hive of activity for shoppers and tourists alike, its impossible not to spend hours on O'Connell street trawling the shops. Dundrum Shopping centre is a 10 minute Luas ride away and is one of the best shopping centres in Ireland. If shopping is the main highlight of your trip, Dublin is the only place to be!
